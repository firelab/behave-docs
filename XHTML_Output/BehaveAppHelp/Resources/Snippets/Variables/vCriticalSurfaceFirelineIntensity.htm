Critical Surface Fireline Intensity

behaveplus:crown:output:fire_type:transition_to_crown_fire:critical_surface_fireline_intensity:help

Critical Surface Fireline Intensity is an intermediate value that estimates the rate of heat transfer per unit of length at the flaming front, that is required for a surface fire to transition to a crown fire (fire moves from crown to crown). It is calculated from Foliar Moisture and Canopy Base Height.

Critical Surface Fireline Intensity is Used to Calculate

  • Transition Ratio

  • Fire Type (indirectly through Transition Ratio)
