Maximum Spotting Distance from Active Crown Fire

behaveplus:crown:output:spotting_active_crown_fire:maximum_spotting_distance:maximum_spotting_distance:help

This distance represents the maximum spotting distance from firebrands produced by an active crown fire in a conifer forest. The spotting distance is based on canopy height, flame length, and wind speed.

  • The maximum spotting distance from an active crown fire is only displayed as an output if active crowning is predicted in Behave.

  • This model is not appropriate for eucalypt forests due to its unique bark characteristics.

  • This model does not calculate potential spotting distances from fire whirls.

    Refer to (Albini et al. 2012) and (Alexander and Cruz 2014) for more information.
