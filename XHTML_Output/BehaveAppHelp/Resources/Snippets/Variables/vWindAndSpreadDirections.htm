Wind and Spread Directions

Spread Direction is the direction in which rate of spread and flame length are calculated. It can be heading, backing, flanking, or somewhere in between.

There are three input options.

  • Heading only: Fire is spreading in the direction of maximum spread.

  • Heading, Flanking, and Backing:

  • Heading fire is moving in the direction of maximum spread.

  • Flanking fire is defined mathematically as fire spread that is 90o from the head at a point on the widest part of the ellipse.

  • Backing fire is fire spread that is 180o from the head.

  • User-defined directions: Users can select any direction of spread along the perimeter.

Direction of Maximum Spread is always calculated for the head of the fire, regardless of the selection made for the remainder of the calculations.

Spread Direction is Used to Calculate

  • Spread Distance

  • Fire Length-Width Ratio

  • Fire Area

  • Fire Perimeter
