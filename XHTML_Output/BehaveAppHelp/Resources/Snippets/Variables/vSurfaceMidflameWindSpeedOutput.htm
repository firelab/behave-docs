Midflame Wind Speed

behaveplus:surface:output:wind-and-fuel:wind:midflame-eye-level-wind-speed:help

The technical definition of the midflame wind speed is the average wind speed from the top of the surface fuel bed to the tip of the flame. It represents the wind speed midway between the surface fuels and the flame height. To calculate 20-Foot or 10-Meter Wind Speed, divide the Midflame Wind Speed by the Wind Adjustment Factor (WAF).

20-Ft Wind Speed = Midflame Wind Speed / WAF

Example: 20-ft Wind Speed = 6 mph / 0.4 = 15 mph
