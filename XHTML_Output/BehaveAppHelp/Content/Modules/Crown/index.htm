Crown Fire

The Surface/Crown module includes additional outputs that are not available in the Surface module. Fire Type is an automated output in this module. The outputs for fire type include surface, torching (passive crown fire), and active. Other outputs, such as rate of spread and flame length, are based on the fire type. Behave 7 uses the same crown fire code as FlamMap, so results in B7 are more consistent with Basic Fire Behavior outputs in FlamMap, IFTDSS, and WFDSS NextGen.

For a surface fire to transition to some type of crown fire, the predicted flame length must be greater than the critical surface flame length.

  • The Transition Ratio output can be used to evaluate surface fire versus some type of crown fire.
  • The rate of spread must be greater than critical crown rate of spread for a fire to be maintained in the tree canopy as an active crown fire.
  • The Active Ratio output can be used to evaluate torching versus active crown fire.

There are two spotting models available in Surface/Crown.

  • The maximum spotting distance from torching trees model is only available in this module.

  • The maximum spotting distance from an active crown fire model is available in Behave and is based on research from Albini (refer to Albini et al. 2012 and Alexander and Cruz 2014). This spotting model is not available in FlamMap, IFTDSS, or WFDSS NextGen.

Crown Fire Assumptions and Limitations

  1. The model is based on eight spread observations for 7 active crown fires in the western U.S., mostly in the Northern Rockies. These fires burned under unstable conditions; therefore, crown fire calculations do not consider the stability of the atmosphere.

  2. Assumes a wind-driven crown fire.

  3. The model assumes that weather conditions do not change.

  4. The crown fire model is not influenced by slope or aspect. Changes in slope only affect surface fire calculations that drive transition to a crown fire.

  5. Does not predict column collapse.
