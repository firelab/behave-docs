Canopy Bulk Density (CBD) describes the mass of canopy fuel per unit of canopy volume. The default unit in Behave is metric (kg/m3) as that is what is reported in the scientific literature and is the default unit in LANDFIRE data.

CBD is used to calculate the critical crown fire rate of spread, which ultimately predicts if the canopy can sustain an active crown fire.

In the photo on the left, there is spacing between each tree canopy and it would be difficult for this stand to sustain an active crown fire as it has a low CBD. The photo on the right has multiple age classes. There was a previous timber harvest on the left and the younger trees are not as continuous as the mature stand on the right. The stand on the left has a lower CBD than the stand on the right. The mature stand could sustain active crown fire under the right conditions.
