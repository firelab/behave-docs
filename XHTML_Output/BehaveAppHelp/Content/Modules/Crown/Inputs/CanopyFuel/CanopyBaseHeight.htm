Canopy Base Height

behaveplus:crown:input:canopy_fuel:canopy_base_height:canopy_base_height:help

Canopy Base Height (CBH) refers to the distance from the ground to the lowest height in a stand of trees with enough canopy fuel to carry fire vertically into the canopy.

  • Canopy base height includes the main canopy and ladder fuels such as conifer regeneration and tall shrubs. Ladder fuels increase vertical continuity and may stimulate transition to a crown fire.

  • CBH is a critical input for determining if a surface fire will transition to a crown fire.

In some forests, the CBH can change as a result of activity or disturbance history. A thinning from below that removes understory trees will often increase the CBH.
