Maximum Spotting Distance from Active Crown Fire

behaveplus:crown:output:spotting_active_crown_fire:maximum_spotting_distance:maximum_spotting_distance:help

This distance represents the maximum spotting distance from firebrands produced by an active crown fire in a conifer forest. The spotting distance is based on canopy height, flame length, and wind speed.

  • The maximum spotting distance from an active crown fire is only displayed as an output if active crowning is predicted in Behave.

  • This model is not appropriate for eucalypt forests due to its unique bark characteristics.

  • This model does not calculate potential spotting distances from fire whirls.

    Refer to (Albini et al. 2012) and (Alexander and Cruz 2014) for more information.

Max Spot Dist from Torching Trees

behaveplus:crown:output:spotting_active_crown_fire:maximum_spotting_distance:max-spot-dist-from-torching-trees:help

Spotting distance from torching trees is the maximum distance an ember is likely to travel and ignite a spot fire. Most embers land much closer to the fire.

This spotting model is available for 14 conifer species. This model provides a maximum spotting distance for embers produced from torching trees, but does not evaluate whether those embers land in burnable fuel.

This model does not apply to extreme situations where spotting may occur up to tens of miles from the fire perimeter. The model does not model spotting from an active crown fire (see Maximum Spotting Distance from an Active Crown Fire).
