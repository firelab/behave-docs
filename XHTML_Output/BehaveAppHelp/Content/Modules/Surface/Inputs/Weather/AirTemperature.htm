Air Temperature is the ambient dry bulb temperature measured in the shade. It can be calculated locally or estimated using measurements from a remote automated weather station (RAWS). A RAWS typically measures Air Temperature at 4 to 8 feet (1.2 to 2.4 m).

Air Temperature is Used to Calculate

  • Reference Fuel Moisture (1-hr Fuel Moisture tool)

  • Probability of Ignition (Ignite tool, Surface, Surface/Mortality)

  • Scorch Height (Surface/Mortality)
