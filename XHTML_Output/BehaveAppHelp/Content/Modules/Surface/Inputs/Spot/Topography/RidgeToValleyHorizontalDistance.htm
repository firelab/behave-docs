Ridge-to-Valley Horizontal Distance

behaveplus:surface:input:spot:topography:ridge_to_valley_horizontal_distance:ridge_to_valley_horizontal_distance:help behaveplus:crown:input:spotting:topography:ridge_to_valley_horizontal_distance:ridge_to_valley_horizontal_distance:help

Ridge-To-Valley Horizontal Distance is the horizontal distance between the ridge top and the valley bottom in the direction of spotting. If winds are blowing directly up- or down-valley, the Ridge-To-Valley Horizontal Distance is likely zero.

Ridge-To-Valley Elevation Distance is Used to Calculate

  • Maximum Spotting Distance from a Burning Pile

  • Maximum Spotting Distance from Torching Trees

  • Maximum Spotting Distance from a Wind-Driven Surface Fire
