Ridge-to-Valley Elevation Difference

behaveplus:surface:input:spot:topography:ridge_to_valley_elevation_difference:ridge_to_valley_elevation_difference:help behaveplus:crown:input:spotting:topography:ridge_to_valley_elevation_difference:ridge_to_valley_elevation_difference:help

Ridge-To-Valley Elevation Distance is the vertical elevation difference between the ridge top and valley bottom in the direction of spotting.

If Ridge-To-Valley Horizontal Distance is zero, the Ridge-To-Valley Elevation Distance should be set to zero.

Ridge-To-Valley Elevation Distance is Used to Calculate

  • Maximum Spotting Distance from Torching Trees

  • Maximum Spotting Distance from a Burning Pile

  • Maximum Spotting Distance from a Wind-Driven Surface Fire
