Resource Arrival Time

Resource Arrival Time is the time that has elapsed from when the fire was reported until the containment resource arrives at the fire and begins fireline construction. All activities such as scouting and briefing that occur before line construction begins are included in Resource Arrival Time.

Resource Line Production Rate

Resource Line Production Rate is the average, sustained rate at which a resource can build and hold fireline. The contain model divides the production rate equally between the two flanks of the fire. For example, a tractor-plow constructs fireline on flat ground at a rate of 120 ch/h. The model assumes that the tractor-plow constructs line at 60 ch/h on each flank.

Guidelines for Resource Line Production Rates were first developed in the 1970s, and rates for hand crews were updated in 2011. Use better data for line production rates if locally available and document assumptions for any of the changes made in the “Notes” section of the worksheet.

Resource Duration (CONTAIN)

Duration is the length of time that a given containment resource can sustain its line production rate for the entire shift. A resource begins line construction at the specified Resource Arrival Time, builds line at its specified Resource Line Production Rate, and stops building line when its Resource Duration has been met. Resources are added to the model based on arrival time until the fire is contained or the fire is predicted to have escaped regardless of resources still in travel status.
