Resource Line Production Rate

Resource Line Production Rate is the average, sustained rate at which a resource can build and hold fireline. The contain model divides the production rate equally between the two flanks of the fire. For example, a tractor-plow constructs fireline on flat ground at a rate of 120 ch/h. The model assumes that the tractor-plow constructs line at 60 ch/h on each flank.

Guidelines for Resource Line Production Rates were first developed in the 1970s, and rates for hand crews were updated in 2011. Use better data for line production rates if locally available and document assumptions for any of the changes made in the “Notes” section of the worksheet.
