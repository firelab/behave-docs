Fire Perimeter (SURFACE, CROWN)

Fire Perimeter is the perimeter of a surface fire that starts from a point source. It assumes the fire has an elliptical shape and there is no suppression. Calculation of Fire Perimeter is calculated from Rate of Spread, Elapsed Time, and wind speed. In SURFACE, Effective Wind Speed is used. In CROWN, 20-ft Wind Speed is used.

Length-to-Width Ratio (SURFACE, CROWN)

Length-to-Width Ratio describes the appearance of an elliptically shaped fire. Fires burning under higher wind speeds have a larger length-to-width ratio and thus will be long and narrow. It is affected by both wind speed and slope in a surface fire, which are combined to calculate the effective wind speed. Slope is not factored into the length-to-width ratio for a crown fire; the only input is 20-ft wind speed.

It is calculated as Heading Spread Distance plus the Backing Spread Distance divided by the Flanking Spread Distance.
